# MOVE 2: HYPOTHESIS

## Project Title

Concept Check: Detecting the Boundary Between Memorization and Understanding

## Problem Statement

Many learners can correctly define technical concepts such as APIs, Interfaces, Databases, and Authentication. However, definitions alone do not guarantee understanding.

A learner may know what a concept is but fail to explain why it exists, what problem it solves, or what would break if it were removed.

Current learning systems primarily evaluate recall rather than conceptual reasoning.

---

## Hypothesis

Learners who can correctly define a technical concept cannot always derive why the concept exists.

If learners are asked progressively deeper reasoning questions, conceptual gaps become visible that are not detectable through standard definition-based assessments.

---

## Success Criteria

A significant portion of learners who correctly answer a definition-based question will fail a derivation-based follow-up question designed to test deeper understanding.

---

## Result That Proves Me Right

More than 70% of participants who correctly define a concept are unable to fully explain why the concept exists or predict what breaks when it is removed.

---

## Result That Means I Built a Horoscope

Participants consistently succeed on both definition-based and derivation-based questions, indicating that the follow-up questions do not reveal any meaningful conceptual gaps.

---

## Kill Number

If fewer than 30% of learners demonstrate a detectable conceptual gap, the hypothesis is considered invalid and the tool does not provide meaningful diagnostic value.

---

## Initial Evidence

The first two API interviews already suggest that learners with similar definitions may possess very different levels of understanding when asked to reason about system behavior and architectural decisions.

This observation motivates the development of a diagnostic system capable of identifying and classifying conceptual understanding levels.
